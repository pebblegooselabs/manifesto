Manifesto
We believe that neutrality in the face of neutrality in the face of documented harm is a mathematical impossibility. To witness a violation and withhold the data is to facilitate the outcome. We believe that peace is the product of transparency, and justice is the residue of truth. We do not seek permission to observe the public record; we view the light of scrutiny as the only effective disinfectant.
We reject the following as acts of systemic obscurity
Complexity as a Shield
We reject jargon to gatekeep public information. If a process cannot be easily accessed and understood by the community it affects, it is designed to exploit them.
The Bystander's Comfort
We reject the passive observation of the spectator. To stand by while evidence disappears is an invitation for trails to go cold.
Institutional Blindness
We reject the safety of the status quo. To ignore data because it contradicts power is a betrayal of the craft.
False Neutrality
We reject the idea that there are two sides to a contaminated well. Factual reality does not require a middle ground, but a witness.
We adhere to total accountability
The Unfiltered Voice
We believe in amplifying the data that the vulnerable cannot safely speak, providing a factual shield for those who can only whisper and for those who cannot defend themselves.
The Open-Source Vow
We release our tools and our findings to everyone. We do not hoard information or methodology — we distribute it as a weapon to the disempowered.
The Mandate of Inquiry
We do not wait for permission to know the truth. We demand it, legally and relentlessly. We view the work as our primary instruments of discovery.
Radical Transparency
We hide nothing about our methods. If our research is sound, it can survive the scrutiny of any adversary.
The Long Game
We do not chase headlines. We build cases. The truth does not expire.
The only peace worth having
Silence is the accomplice of complicity. We will not be silent.
Courage and selflessness are not just qualities, but discipline.
There is no integrity without transparency, and no resolution without facts.
What We Do

Two pillars.
One methodology.

01
Financial Fraud & Accountability
We trace money through IRS 990 filings, FEC campaign finance data, state lobbying registries, corporate filings, and FOIA responses. We map shell company networks, document grant fraud, and surface undisclosed lobbying coordination. We specialize in the systematic reconstruction of financial events using authenticated public records.
02
Environmental Pollution & Enforcement Gaps
We document permit violations, illegal discharge incidents, contamination events, and regulatory failures using EPA ECHO, state environmental databases, satellite imagery, and geospatial analysis. We do not take positions on policy. We document what happened, whether enforcement followed, and who bears the burden.

About

What PGL is.

Pebble Goose Labs is an independent public interest research organization based in Florida. We investigate financial fraud and environmental pollution using open-source methodology and publicly available records — and publish everything freely.

We are not a journalism outlet. We are not a law firm. We are not an advocacy organization. We are investigative infrastructure — the connective tissue between raw public records and the communities, lawyers, journalists, and regulators who need them.

All tools are released under AGPL licensing. All findings are published openly. No one pays us to find out what their government is doing. That is the point.

PGL does not advocate. PGL does not lobby. PGL does not tell anyone what to do with what it finds. PGL follows the records. Regulators, courts, and the public decide what comes next.
