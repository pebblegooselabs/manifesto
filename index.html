Manifesto
We believe that Diomedes was the true aristos archaion, the best of the Greeks. While Achilles was defined by his rage and Agamemnon by his ambition and Odysseus by his cunning, Diomedes was defined by his balance: the martial prowess of a warrior and the discipline of a thinker. In that spirit, we are committed to the pursuit of knowledge with both rigor and humility. We understand that the world is complex and that uncovering hidden realities requires not only technical skill but also ethical clarity. Our manifesto is a declaration of our principles and a shared commitment to the work we do.
We reject the following
Jargon as a Shield
To obfuscate information is to prevent understanding. If a process cannot be easily accessed and understood by the community it affects, it is designed to exploit them.
Bystanding
To stand by while evidence disappears is an invitation for trails to go cold. Silence in the face of documented harm is an act of complicity.
Institutional Blindness
To ignore data because it contradicts institutional interests is a betrayal of the public trust. If the data exists, it must be acknowledged.
False Neutrality
To claim there are two sides to a contaminated well is to deny the existence of the contamination. Factual reality only requires a witness.
We adhere to the following deeds
Witnessing the Record
We document what can be verified. Where evidence exists but attention does not, we ensure it is seen.
Vowing to Share
We release our tools and our findings to everyone. Knowledge is most valuable when it is accessible.
Requesting the Truth
We demand the truth, legally and persistently. The record is the foundation of all claims.
Standing for Transparency
We hide nothing about our methods. If our research is sound, it can survive any scrutiny.
Playing the Long Game
We do not chase headlines. We build cases. The truth does not expire.

What We Do

Four pillars.
One mission.

01
Funding & Financial Traceability
We trace how money moves through public and regulated systems. Using SEC filings, IRS Form 990s, procurement records, corporate registries, and other public disclosures, we map how funds are allocated, distributed, and ultimately controlled. We identify ownership structures, intermediary entities, and financial pathways that connect funding to real-world systems.
02
Infrastructure Verification & Ground Truth
We verify whether funded systems exist and function as claimed. Using satellite imagery, geospatial analysis, permitting data, and on-the-ground indicators, we compare stated outcomes against physical reality. If a project was funded, we determine what was actually built, where it exists, and whether it aligns with its intended purpose.
03
Control, Ownership & System Risk
We identify who ultimately controls the systems that public and regulated funds support. By connecting financial records, corporate structures, and operational control, we surface patterns of influence, repeated actors, and structural risks. We do not speculate. We document control as it appears in the public record and assess the implications of that control.
04
Open Data & Investigative Infrastructure
We publish our findings, datasets, and methodologies openly. Our goal is not only to conduct investigations, but to make them reproducible. By structuring data and standardizing our methods, we enable nonprofits, researchers, journalists, and communities to verify systems independently and build on our work.
