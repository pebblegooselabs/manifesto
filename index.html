What We Do

Four pillars.
One mission.

01
Financial Fraud & Accountability
We trace money through SEC filings, IRS 990 filings, federal oversight reports, state and federal lobbying registries, corporate filings, and FOIA requests. We map shell company networks, document grant fraud, surface undisclosed lobbying coordination, among others. If it moved through the financial system and left a public record, we will find it.
02
Environmental Pollution & Enforcement Gaps
We document permit violations, illegal discharge incidents, contamination events, and regulatory failures using EPA ECHO, state environmental databases, satellite imagery, geospatial analysis, etc. We do not take positions on policy. We document what happened, whether enforcement followed, and who bears the burden.
03
Data Distribution & Public Access
We make our data accessible to the public through open-source platforms and user-friendly interfaces. Our commitment is to ensure that the information we gather is available for scrutiny, analysis, and use by anyone interested in transparency and accountability.
04
Network Cultivation & Community Training
We build the human infrastructure for open source intelligence research by training local witnesses to identify, document, and report institutional misconduct. We also cultivate a network of volunteer researchers to assist with data collection, analysis, and distribution. We do not rely on insiders or whistleblowers to come forward; we empower everyday people to be the eyes and ears of their communities.
